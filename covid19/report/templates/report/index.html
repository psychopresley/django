The world in numbers


One way to visualize the impact of the Sars-Cov-2 outbreak in the world is to divide it into geopolitical regions. It is natural to assume that the numbers are higher in the regions with higher economic activity and trade level with China, where the first cases of infection were reported. Here are the numbers by geopolitical region as defined by the WHO.

I. Daily cases


In the map below each pulse represents the confirmed cases in the country. The larger the pulse is the higher is the number of infections confirmed that day. This gives us the idea of how fast the virus has spread and also the intensity of the wave in each country. The counter sums up the total confirmed cases per day. The map is interactive so you can change the period for the counter and also if you're interested in the rate of spread or the aggregated numbers.

II. Scaling down to a thousand people

Now imagine that the total number of people infected by the Sars-cov-2 virus since the beginning of the outbreak is scaled down to a group of 1000 people gathered together in a room. This would be the distribution of cases according to the region of origin of the members in that group. Select the case you're interested in and hover the mouse over the map below to visualize the numbers by region.

III. Deaths


The graphic below shows the mortality rate of the covid-19 by each country as a deaths/confirmed ratio. The graphic is interactive so hover over the dots, select a specific label for zooming and explore the infos. The numbers of Iemen, with an ongoing civil war are devastating. More than a quarter of the infected dies. The top 5 is composed by the poorest countries of the eastern mediterranean and latin america

IV. Top countries by active cases


The key indicator of the coronavirus outbreak is the number of daily active cases. This is the total number of confirmed cases, minus the reported number of deaths, minus the total recovered people in a given day. In other words it is the current number of people with the coronavirus disease. The chart below shows the top 10 countries by active cases through time.

V. Tops and bottoms ...and the in betweens

Each country has performed differently in the fight against the coronavirus, depending on several factors such as political decisions, climate, war, HDI and social behaviour. The chart below separates all countries according to the chosen aspect. For example, in the 'shade by' menu select the "active cases interval" option and you'll see which countries have few or high cases. The 'median with quartiles' method is used here to define the "high/few" cases and all cases are taken as a percentage of the total of confirmed infections. You can also group countries and compare them using any criteria at your disposal, so feel free to explore it!
